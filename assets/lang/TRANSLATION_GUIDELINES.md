# Style & Content Guide

---

## Foundations
Keep in mind this game isn't meant to be soulless, but it isn't meant to be recklessly written either.
### Target Contributors
Assume all players are of legal age and is into furry inflation.
### Knowledge Level
Assume players have **basic gaming literacy** but no technical knowledge of game development. For _Utility Menus_ (e.g. Character Editor), assume users have basic understanding of **animation processes and file organization.**

Translators should be **proficient** in their target language and **understand localization conventions.**
### Tone & Voice
The **tone and meaning** of the translations should remain **uniform.** All **UI text** should be delivered in a **technical but informal** tone. Unless said otherwise, if it's at least **three sentences long**, you may switch to a **witty tone** for comedic effect. **Cultural differences** in humor may vary from region to region, so you should consider that too.

---

## Voice, Tone and Context
Technical for small UI elements, witty and humorous for lengthy prompts and flavor text.

| Context                            | Tone               | Examples / Guidelines                                                                                |
|------------------------------------|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Game UI**                        | Technical, Compact | **Do:** Select Target <br>**Don't:** Who do you want to apply this on?                               |
| **Lengthy Prompts & Descriptions** | Witty, Natural     | **Do:** Make jokes in local context<br>**Don't:** Force punchlines in that cannot be translated      |
| **Instructions**                   | Clear & Helpful    | **Do:** Press any key to exit<br>**Don't:** If you wanna leave, just press any key on your keyboard! |

### Writing
#### Tone Consistency
Match the original tone in translations. If it's humorous in the source, make it humorous in the translation.

For inherently unserious languages like LOLCAT or Pirate Speak, prioritize message clarity over strict tone matching.
#### Cultural Adaptation
Regional jokes that lose meaning should be replaced with equivalent local humor or rewritten entirely if necessary.
#### Message Integrity
The core message must remain unchanged across all translations to avoid player confusion.

---

## Formatting
Standardizing formatting prevents layout inconsistencies and maintains readability.

### Capitalization
Follow target language conventions for UI elements and proper nouns. If it's fully capitalized in the source, follow it.
### Naming
Proper names of people and places remain untranslated.

For non-Latin scripts, transliterate phonetically to match English pronunciation, except for the terms "Inflation Roulette" and "Reloaded".
### Commenting
Comments use double slashes (//) at the start of each line and will be ignored by the game.

Translation notes and instructions in comments are not translated and should be ignored by the game.

---

## Inclusivity
Everyone from Mexico to Russia to Hong Kong China is gonna play this game. Ensure all users feel respected.

## Try To Not Break Down
If you think you're not doing good enough, remember it isn't your fault.
Keep in mind that this is a collaborative project and no one owns one singular language to translate.

You're doing me, the developer, Ajuñiga, God of Linguistics, and the community a favor.
